Sim2Real

Bridging Simulation and Reality Across Disciplines

NeurIPS 2026 Workshop · December 2026 (date TBA) · Atlanta (TBC)

Shared principles for learning in simulation and transferring to the real world.

About

Modern decision-making systems are trained in simulation, where data is cheap and exploration is safe — but the deployment target is always the real world. The reality gap is the central obstacle, and it is not owned by any single field. Sim2Real treats it as one methodological problem: how to build approximate simulators, how to layer machine learning on top of them, and how to measure transfer in a way that predicts real-world robustness.

We anchor the discussion in robotics and the physical sciences, and stress-test the common principles against four exemplar domains: energy and industrial control, computing systems, healthcare, and scientific discovery.

Three driving questions

  1. How to leverage scale effectively.
  2. How to formalize metrics of transfer that predict real-world robustness.
  3. What underexplored opportunities cross-disciplinary exchange can reveal.
